Research

Advancing systems biology and machine learning for biological discoveries

Transcriptional Regulatory Network Reconstruction

Systems biology has benefited from improvements in omics technologies, leading to an interest in the comprehensive molecular processes that bacteria go through in response to their surroundings.

Approach

By specifically utilizing Chromatin immunoprecipitation (ChIP-seq) and transcriptomics analysis (RNA-seq), we can reconstruct a transcriptional regulatory network (TRN) that offers insight into gene regulation and interaction in response to external stimuli.

Target Organisms

  • Escherichia coli
  • Salmonella enterica
  • Klebsiella species
  • Methylobacterium

Tools & Methods

To conduct the high-throughput experiment data analysis, we have developed a specific pipeline and deep-learning-based high-performance software for TRN reconstruction and analysis.

Genome-Scale Metabolic Modeling

Genome-scale models (GSMs) are knowledge-based and experimentally validated mathematical representations of biological metabolism. Utilizing GSMs can provide insights into how biological systems function and help in the design of microbial cell factories.

Research Topics

  • Genome-scale model reconstruction of high-value bacterial strains including Streptomyces, Vibrio, and C1 bacteria
  • Model-based analysis to understand complex biological systems or to design microbial cell factories
  • Omics data integration for biological discovery

Applications

Our models are used for metabolic engineering, pathway optimization, and predicting cellular behavior under various environmental conditions.

Characterization of High-Value Bacterial Strains

We focus on understanding and optimizing industrially important bacterial strains for biotechnology applications.

Key Activities

  • Metabolic pathway analysis and optimization
  • Strain improvement through systems-level understanding
  • Production of valuable compounds and enzymes
  • Integration of multi-omics data for comprehensive characterization

Anti-Microbial Resistance

Investigating mechanisms of antimicrobial resistance and developing strategies to combat resistant pathogens using systems biology approaches.

Research Focus

  • Understanding resistance mechanisms at the systems level
  • Identifying novel drug targets through network analysis
  • Predicting resistance evolution
  • Developing combination therapy strategies

Machine Learning for Biological Data

Developing and applying advanced machine learning frameworks for analyzing biological information and solving complex problems.

Applications

  • Deep learning for transcriptomics data analysis
  • Enzyme function prediction using AI models
  • Protein engineering through machine learning
  • Automated biological discovery from large-scale datasets

Machine Learning for Energy Systems

Energy storage with lithium-ion batteries have been widely used for power sources due to their high energy density and convenience for management.

Battery Systems

We are constructing general-purpose deep-learning frameworks for battery prognostics and health management, including:

  • State of Health (SoH) estimation
  • Remaining Useful Life (RUL) prediction
  • Configuration-agnostic models using Transformers

Hydrogen Production

Our research extends to energy generation systems such as hydrogen production. We study machine-learning frameworks for predicting the feasibility of hydrogen production systems, particularly in methanol steam reforming with membrane filters.
